About Me

Short Bio (Quick overview)

João Melo is a Brazilian percussionist and educator pursuing a double major in Percussion Performance and Philosophy at Bard College in New York. A first-generation college student from Salvador, Bahia, João began his journey in the music and social project NEOJIBA, where he developed a deep commitment to music as a tool for empowerment and transformation.

At Bard, João performs in orchestras, chamber ensembles, and solo settings, mentors young musicians, and works in audiovisual and event production. He is known for blending classical repertoire with Afro-Brazilian traditions and has led workshops exploring the historical and spiritual roots of Ijexá and Samba Reggae.

In 2025, João was selected as a fellow of the OAcademy Orchestra Institute. His story is marked by persistence, creativity, and a community-centered vision. His senior project connects philosophy, education, and music, and he is developing a community initiative linking youth and elders through rhythm and storytelling.

Full Bio (Complete version)

João Melo is a Brazilian percussionist, educator, and interdisciplinary thinker currently pursuing a double major in Percussion Performance and Philosophy at Bard College in New York. As a first-generation international student on a full scholarship, João’s work lies at the intersection of music, community empowerment, and reflective inquiry.

He began his musical journey in Salvador, Bahia, through the social project NEOJIBA, where he discovered how music could be a powerful tool for transformation. His background in Afro-Brazilian rhythms and classical training informs a style rooted in both precision and communal experience.

At Bard, João performs extensively, mentors young musicians, and works in production roles. He has led workshops on Afro-Brazilian rhythms like Ijexá and Samba Reggae. In 2025, João was selected for the OAcademy Orchestra Institute, years after an earlier rejection led him down the path that brought him to Bard.

His senior project bridges the thought of Hannah Arendt and Paulo Freire with cultural knowledge from Salvador’s percussion scene. João is currently developing a community-based project in Salvador connecting youth and elders through percussion and storytelling. Fluent in English and Portuguese, he envisions art as a path to justice and healing.
